The data consists of:

  • a set of zero-variate data (i.e. a set of numbers)
  • and, possibly, one or more sets of uni-variate data (each consisting of list of values for the independent and the associated dependent variable).

Zero-variate data sets have real and pseudo data points. Pseudo-data sets are (simple functions of) parameter values corresponding to a generalised animal, i.e. typical values for a wide variety of animals (Lika at al 2011). Pseudo-data serves to fill possible gaps in information that is contained in the real data. Only intensive parameters can play the role of pseudo-data points. Since the value for specific cost for structure (E_G) is sensitive for the water content of tissue, which differs between jelly fish and vertebrates, it is replaced it by the growth efficiency kap_G.

Real data relates to actual observations on the species of interest at specified temperatures and food conditions. Pseudo-data relates to the generalised animal at the reference temperature.

Generally the use of statistics derived from observations, such as the von Bertalanffy growth rate or the half saturation coefficient, as data from which DEB parameters are estimates is discouraged. It is far better to base the parameter estimation directly on the measurements, avoiding manipulation or interpretation. For instance, if wet weights were measured, use wet weights as data and do not convert them first to dry weights (or vice versa).

Length data should be supplemented for conversion to weights, to eliminate effects of shape. Notice that times and rates without temperature are meaningless. Allocation fraction kappa can only be estimated if data contains information on growth as well as reproduction. Reproduction investment is determined by reproduction rate, in combination with mass at birth, and mass at puberty, to quantify losses via maturity maintenance. Is information can not only be directly, but also indirectly available in data. The weight can be dry, ash-free dry or wet weights; these weight relate to each other via specific densities and chemical indices. Assuming that the specific density of wet mass is close to 1 g/cm3, check the values for d_V and d_E that refer to dry weight.
