Dear DEB list members,

Apart from rather frequent incidental courses/workshops here and there, the "regular" DEB course history is
listed in the DEBcalendar: https://debportal.debtheory.org/docs/DEBcalendar.html

1993 yearly DEB courses at VU Univ just after DEB1 was published
2001 2-yearly international internet courses as soon as VU Univ bought software Backboard
2009 2-yearly courses plus symposium and Special Issue as we have them now, with help of Marianne Alunno-Bruscia and financial support by Ifremer
2018 MOOC course, developed by the Lisbon-group, replacing Blackboard, as preparation for our "classic" DEB courses
     see https://debportal.debtheory.org/docs/DEBevents.html

and now a new chapter, which I see as preparation for or parallel to the MOOC courses: 
2026 DEBlearn: an AI DEB learning tool, set up by Lothar Kuijper at VU Univ using AI tool Replit.

Lothar graduated with me in 2004 on trophic flows in food web dynamics, so he had quite an exposure to early DEB, but he is now specialised in education.
Important gadgets of DEBlearn are that the user can interact in his/her own language (Chinese, Spanish and many more)
and take the learning experience at any time and as frequently as wanted.

DEBlearn is still in the development-stage as part of the Open Education concept of VU Univ, but you can already see it:
as first step goto https://120526-leeromgevingreplit.replit.app/feedback and login to LEAP-VU. 
Select the language of your preference in the top toolbar right.
Select the DEB course lower left ("Switch course", depending on the language that you selected). 

Please play and mail your experience/suggestions to l.d.j.kuijper@vu.nl
Your work will help to improve DEBlearn, after which it will be officially hosted by VU Univ with is more logical address (I hope).
I will by then make links from the DEB website to DEBlearn.

Best wishes

Bas Kooijman
